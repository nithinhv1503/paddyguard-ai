🌿 Paddy Leaf Diseases

Our AI model is trained to identify these common paddy leaf diseases with high accuracy.

High Severity

Bacterial Leaf Blight

Caused by Xanthomonas oryzae, this bacterial disease causes wilting and yellowing of leaves, significantly reducing rice yield.

  • Water-soaked lesions on leaf margins
  • Yellowing and wilting of leaves
  • Grayish-white lesions with wavy edges
  • Stunted plant growth
Medium Severity

Brown Spot

Caused by Bipolaris oryzae, this fungal disease appears as brown oval lesions on leaves, affecting grain quality.

  • Small oval brown lesions on leaves
  • Lesions with gray or white centers
  • Spots may merge to form large patches
  • Infected seeds appear discolored
Low Severity

Leaf Smut

Caused by Entyloma oryzae, this fungal disease produces small black spots on leaves but generally has a minor impact on yield.

  • Small angular black spots on leaves
  • Slightly raised fungal sori
  • Spots scattered across the leaf surface
  • Mild leaf distortion in severe cases
Healthy

Healthy Leaf

A healthy paddy leaf shows vibrant green color with no visible spots, lesions, or discoloration — indicating a disease-free plant.

  • Uniform green coloration
  • No visible spots or lesions
  • Smooth leaf surface
  • Normal growth and vigor
